## 1. Purpose

Welcome to the WeianData Engineering Handbook. It is the engineering operating system for building trustworthy software for trustworthy measurement.

## 2. Scope

The handbook governs company engineering, statistical computing, AI-assisted work, client delivery, security, repository administration, and open-source work. It applies to humans, contractors, and AI agents acting for WeianData.

## 3. Philosophy

Professional measurement is the foundation. Artificial intelligence is an accelerator. Engineering excellence is the commitment. Software is valuable only when its scientific purpose, evidence, and limitations remain understandable and reproducible.

## 4. Principles

1. Engineering serves science.
2. Statistical correctness outweighs implementation convenience.
3. Documentation is part of the product.
4. Engineering knowledge belongs to the company.
5. Reproducibility is non-negotiable.
6. AI augments expertise but does not replace scientific judgment.
7. Simple systems are preferred.
8. Automation should remove repetitive work.
9. Decisions should be explainable.
10. Long-term maintainability outweighs short-term productivity.

## 5. Standards

Readers MUST follow the authority order in the [authoring constitution](../SPECIFICATION/handbook-authoring-rules.md#3-authority-and-precedence). The [handbook README](../README.md) is the canonical reading order and topic index.

When a repository rule conflicts with this handbook, the repository owner MUST either align the repository or record an approved, time-bounded exception. Scientific, security, and client-data requirements MUST NOT be silently relaxed.

## 6. Best Practices

- Begin a task by identifying the applicable handbook chapters.
- Link decisions and review evidence to the governing rule.
- Prefer small, reversible changes with explicit acceptance criteria.
- Capture reusable knowledge after delivery.
- Escalate uncertainty before it becomes an undocumented assumption.

## 7. Examples

### Example: starting a statistical software change

An engineer reads the engineering workflow, repository standards, statistical validation, reproducibility, and AI policy chapters. The pull request links its validation evidence and records any AI contribution. The engineer does not copy those rules into the repository.

## 8. Checklist

- [ ] Applicable chapters have been identified.
- [ ] The authoritative rule is linked rather than duplicated.
- [ ] Scientific, security, data, and reproducibility risks are classified.
- [ ] Required evidence and accountable approval are defined.
- [ ] Reusable knowledge will be preserved.

## 9. Summary

The handbook is the single source of truth for how WeianData engineers, validates, secures, and preserves its work.
