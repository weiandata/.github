## 1. Purpose

This chapter defines respectful, scientifically responsible participation in WeianData public projects.

## 2. Scope

It applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, events, documentation, and other project spaces managed by WeianData.

## 3. Philosophy

Constructive collaboration requires respect, evidence, clear boundaries, and safe reporting paths.

## 4. Principles

- Critique ideas and evidence, not people.
- Welcome questions while preserving scientific rigor.
- Protect privacy, confidentiality, and security.
- Attribute contributions and resolve conflicts transparently.
- Match support commitments to project capacity.

## 5. Standards

Participants MUST communicate respectfully and MUST NOT engage in harassment, discrimination, intimidation, doxxing, deliberate disruption, or unauthorized disclosure. Technical claims SHOULD include reproducible evidence appropriate to the claim.

Public channels MUST NOT contain personal information, client data, credentials, embargoed vulnerabilities, or confidential material. Security reports MUST use the repository's private security contact. Maintainers MUST disclose relevant conflicts, apply project rules consistently, and document moderation actions proportionately.

Contributions MUST follow the repository's contribution, license, and authorship terms. AI-assisted contributions MUST meet the same originality, review, disclosure, and evidence requirements as human-authored work.

## 6. Best Practices

- Provide minimal reproductions with synthetic data.
- Explain why a contribution fits the project's scope.
- Ask clarifying questions before assuming intent.
- Credit reporters, reviewers, and contributors appropriately.
- Move sensitive discussion to the approved private channel promptly.

## 7. Examples

### Example: constructive disagreement

A reviewer states which statistical assumption is unsupported, supplies a reproducible counterexample, and proposes an acceptance test. The comment addresses the method, not the contributor.

## 8. Checklist

- [ ] Communication is respectful, scoped, and evidence-based.
- [ ] No private, client, personal, credential, or vulnerability information is exposed.
- [ ] Contribution and AI-disclosure rules are followed.
- [ ] Conflicts and moderation actions are handled transparently.
- [ ] Security concerns use the private reporting path.

## 9. Summary

WeianData communities combine respectful participation with strong scientific, security, and evidence standards.
