## 5. Semantic review

### Engineering architecture

- The authority chain is explicit and consistent with the master specification.
- Operational topics have a single owning chapter recorded in the handbook specification.
- Cross-domain chapters link to the owning policy rather than attempting to replace it.
- The chapter order matches the six parts and appendix required by the master specification.

### Company alignment

- Engineering is subordinate to measurement and scientific validity.
- AI is treated as an implementation accelerator, not scientific authority.
- The founder-led, AI-assisted operating model retains accountable human approval.
- Reusable software, R and Python workflows, client delivery, and open source are supported without making temporary tool choices constitutional.

### Statistical quality

- Verification and statistical validation are distinguished.
- IRT and measurement validation cover intended use, identification, fit, precision, invariance or DIF, linking, missingness, sensitivity, and uncertainty as applicable.
- Reproducibility, simulation, and benchmarking standards require controlled environments, randomness, failure reporting, and evidence.
- No universal statistical cutoff is asserted without intended-use justification.

### AI quality

- AI tasks require data classification, bounded permissions, verification, and stop conditions.
- AI output is untrusted until reviewed.
- AI agents cannot serve as accountable approvers.
- Material AI contribution and verification are disclosed in pull requests.

### Security and client data

- The default client-delivery architecture is tool-and-data isolation.
- Restricted client data is prohibited from source control and unapproved external AI services.
- Development uses synthetic or minimally permitted de-identified fixtures.
- Client-side tools include integrity, validation, safe logging, and approved-output controls.

### Language and maintainability

- All handbook content is English, concise, and engineering-oriented.
- Normative keywords have controlled meanings.
- Temporary project choices and promotional claims are excluded from operational standards.
- The roadmap favors additive evolution over structural redesign.

## 6. External-source review

Security and AI risk language was calibrated against official primary sources:

- [NIST Secure Software Development Framework](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/218/final)
- [NIST AI Risk Management Framework](https://www.nist.gov/publications/artificial-intelligence-risk-management-framework-ai-rmf-10)
- [OWASP Application Security Verification Standard](https://owasp.org/www-project-application-security-verification-standard/)
- [Personal Information Protection Law of the People's Republic of China](https://en.spp.gov.cn/2021-12/29/c_948419.htm)

The handbook does not claim certification against these sources and does not substitute engineering policy for legal advice.

## 7. Confidentiality review

The handbook contains no raw client data, credentials, private keys, personal contact details, restricted schemas, or non-public client artifacts. The business-plan source was used only to derive stable company context and the approved tool-and-data isolation model.

## 8. Known limitations and owner actions

- External links were reviewed for authority and relevance but are not guaranteed to remain unchanged.
- The initial release has no continuous-integration workflow; the local validator is included and CI adoption is planned for v1.1.
- The local `v1.0.0` tag identifies this release; remote push and GitHub Release creation are outside this local publication.
- Project-specific legal, contractual, security, and statistical review remains required; the handbook cannot pre-approve a future client use case.

## 9. Publication decision

The v1.0.0 document set satisfies the structural, editorial, cross-reference, confidentiality, and coverage requirements of the approved master specification. Publication was explicitly requested by the WeianData repository owner. The release is suitable as the initial company engineering baseline, subject to the project-specific reviews stated above.
